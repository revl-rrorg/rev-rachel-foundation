Who We Are

The Rev & Rachel Foundation

Rev and Rachel met in 1996 at a costume party in Santa Cruz — a film student and a computer‑graphics engineer who discovered they shared the same curiosity and appetite for adventure. They married in New Orleans in 2001, raised two sons across four continents, and built a life rooted in the places they love most: Armenia and New Orleans.

The foundation is the formal expression of that commitment — funding and supporting the people and organizations building better futures in the places they love.

Rev Lebaredian

Co-Founder

VP, Omniverse & Simulation Technology · NVIDIA

Rev Lebaredian is a pioneering technologist whose career has traced the arc of modern computing — from film visual effects and real-time computer graphics to physical AI and next-generation simulation. As Vice President at NVIDIA, he leads Omniverse and Simulation Technology, overseeing a platform that enables engineers, researchers, and creators to build physically accurate virtual worlds used in robotics, autonomous vehicles, industrial design, and scientific research.

His journey began in Hollywood in the 1990s, specializing in rendering at Warner Bros. Digital and Disney's Dream Quest Images. That early work led him to co-found Steamboat Software, building rendering and simulation tools for the visual effects industry. He joined NVIDIA in 2002, where he spent over a decade leading the development of GameWorks — including PhysX, the physics middleware that became the industry standard for video games worldwide — before turning his focus to the far larger challenge of simulating physical reality itself.

A sought-after speaker and author on the convergence of computer graphics and AI, Rev has written and spoken extensively about how simulation technology is enabling breakthroughs in robotics, manufacturing, and scientific discovery. He serves on the boards of TUMO, the American University of Armenia, the Armenian Innovation Foundation, and the Armenian Symphony Orchestra — bringing the same long-term thinking to governance that he applies to engineering.

Born with deep roots in the Armenian community, Rev has channeled his success in technology into a lifelong commitment to Armenia's future — believing that the diaspora's most powerful gift is not money, but belief, and that technology and education are the twin engines of national renewal.

Rachel Lebaredian

Co-Founder

Co-Founder & Director · Rev & Rachel Foundation

Board Member · FOTFA (Friends of Teach For Armenia)

Rachel Lebaredian is the heart of the foundation — a passionate advocate for Armenia's cultural heritage, educational equity, and the potential of the Armenian people. Though not Armenian by birth, her love for Armenia and its people is genuine, deep, and unconditional, shaped by decades of living, traveling, and raising a family with Armenian roots at the center.

Born and raised in Austin, Texas, Rachel grew up with a multicultural appreciation for what it means to carry a heritage — to preserve it, pass it forward, and fight for it when it's threatened. It is perhaps that same instinct that draws her so powerfully to Armenia's story: a small nation with an outsized will to survive, create, and lead.

As Co-Founder and Director of the Rev & Rachel Foundation, Rachel guides the organization's relationships with partners on the ground and ensures the foundation's support reaches communities in ways that are human, responsive, and lasting. As a board member of FOTFA — Friends of Teach For Armenia — she works directly alongside educators and advocates committed to transforming Armenia's schools.

She brings warmth, purpose, and unwavering conviction to the work of nation building — one organization, one teacher, one generation at a time.
