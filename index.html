Who We Are

The Rev & Rachel Foundation

Rev and Rachel met in 1996 at a costume party in Santa Cruz — a film student and a computer‑graphics engineer who quickly discovered they shared the same curiosity, creativity, and appetite for adventure. They married in New Orleans in 2001, raised two sons while living and working across four continents, and have spent the years since building a life deeply rooted in the places they love most: Armenia and New Orleans.

Their connection to Armenia began in 2005 and has only deepened with time — through visits, relationships, and a growing conviction that the country's people, history, and creative energy deserve sustained, meaningful support. New Orleans, where they married and have called home for years, holds the same place in their hearts: a community worth investing in, generation after generation.

The Rev and Rachel Foundation is the formal expression of that commitment — bringing together Rev's work in AI and simulation and Rachel's focus on storytelling, family, and community to fund and support the people and organizations building better futures in the places they love.

Rev Lebaredian

Co-Founder

VP, Omniverse & Simulation Technology · NVIDIA

Rev Lebaredian is a pioneering technologist whose career has traced the arc of modern computing — from real-time computer graphics and film visual effects to physical AI and next-generation simulation. As Vice President at NVIDIA, he leads Omniverse and Simulation Technology, overseeing a platform that enables engineers, researchers, and creators to build physically accurate virtual worlds used in robotics, autonomous vehicles, industrial design, and scientific research.

His journey in technology began in the 1990s, where early work in computer graphics led him to co-found Steamboat Software — building rendering tools that powered major Hollywood productions. He joined NVIDIA in 2002, and over two decades has helped shape the company's vision for simulation and physical AI, championing the idea that the next frontier of intelligence is not just digital, but embodied: machines that understand and navigate the physical world.

A sought-after speaker and author on the convergence of computer graphics and AI, Rev has written and spoken extensively about how simulation technology is enabling breakthroughs in robotics, manufacturing, and scientific discovery. He serves on the boards of TUMO, the American University of Armenia, the Armenian Innovation Foundation, and the Armenian Symphony Orchestra — bringing the same long-term thinking to governance that he applies to engineering.

Born with deep roots in the Armenian community, Rev has channeled his success in Silicon Valley into a lifelong commitment to Armenia's future — believing that technology and education are the twin engines of national renewal, and that the diaspora's most powerful gift is not money, but belief.

Rachel Lebaredian

Co-Founder

Co-Founder & Director · Rev & Rachel Foundation

Board Member · FOTFA (Friends of Teach For Armenia)

Rachel Lebaredian is the heart of the foundation — a passionate advocate for Armenia's cultural heritage, educational equity, and the potential of the Armenian people. Though not Armenian herself, her love for Armenia and its people is genuine, deep, and unconditional.

Born and raised in Austin, Texas, Rachel comes from a rich tapestry of American roots — a multicultural upbringing that gave her an early appreciation for what it means to carry a heritage, preserve it, and pass it forward.

It is perhaps that same understanding that draws her so powerfully to Armenia's story. She brings warmth, purpose, and unwavering conviction to the work of nation building, one organization and one generation at a time.
