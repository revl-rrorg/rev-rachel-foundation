Rev and Rachel's journey began in 1996 at a costume party in Santa Cruz, California, where a film student and a young computer graphics engineer met and quickly connected over creativity, technology, and a shared curiosity about the world. After graduation, Rachel moved to Los Angeles to join Rev, and together they immersed themselves in projects that fused storytelling with emerging visual technologies — the earliest expression of a partnership that would come to unite imagination, innovation, and purpose.

In 1998, Rev and Rachel founded Steamboat Software. Three years later, they were married in New Orleans, a city whose cultural depth, artistic vitality, and strong sense of community left a lasting imprint on their lives. In 2002, Rev joined NVIDIA, beginning a global chapter that carried the family from Los Angeles to Austin, then to Moscow, Shanghai, and ultimately to Los Gatos, California. Living across different places and cultures deepened their belief that a child's future is shaped not only by talent, but by access, belonging, and the strength of the community around them — a belief made even more personal with the births of their sons, Daron and Zareh, during their years in Moscow.

Beginning in 2005, Rev and Rachel also started traveling regularly to Armenia. Over time, those visits became far more than family trips. They evolved into enduring relationships with educators, artists, innovators, and nonprofit leaders, and into a profound sense of connection and responsibility to a place that came to feel like home. Armenia and New Orleans gradually emerged as the twin anchors of their philanthropic vision: places of extraordinary resilience, creativity, and cultural richness, where investment in young people can have a lasting and transformative impact.

In 2023, they founded the Rev and Rachel Foundation to turn that vision into sustained action. The foundation reflects the values that have guided their life together: creativity, curiosity, generosity, and a deep belief in human potential. Drawing on Rev's leadership in AI, simulation, and technological innovation, and Rachel's commitment to family, storytelling, and community, the foundation advances educational opportunity, creative and STEM learning, and youth empowerment in Armenia, New Orleans, and the communities they hold close.

Rev Lebaredian

Co-Founder

VP, Omniverse & Simulation Technology · NVIDIA

LinkedIn

Rev Lebaredian is a pioneering technologist whose career reflects the evolution of modern computing — from film visual effects and real-time graphics to physical AI and advanced simulation. As Vice President of Omniverse and Simulation Technology at NVIDIA, he leads the development of a platform that enables engineers, researchers, and creators to build physically accurate virtual worlds used in robotics, autonomous vehicles, industrial design, and scientific research.

He began his career in Hollywood in the 1990s, specializing in rendering at Warner Bros. Digital and Disney's Dream Quest Images, before co-founding Steamboat Software to create rendering and simulation tools for the visual effects industry. Since joining NVIDIA in 2002, he has helped shape some of the company's most influential technologies, including GameWorks and PhysX — physics middleware that became a global standard in gaming — before turning to the broader challenge of simulating the physical world itself.

Rev is also a widely respected speaker and writer on the convergence of AI, graphics, and simulation, and on how these technologies are transforming robotics, manufacturing, and scientific discovery. Beyond his work in technology, he serves on the boards of TUMO, the American University of Armenia, the Armenian Innovation Foundation, and the Armenian Symphony Orchestra, reflecting a long-standing commitment to education, culture, and innovation.

Grounded in his Armenian heritage, Rev has dedicated both his professional expertise and philanthropic energy to Armenia's future. He believes that technology and education are powerful engines of national renewal, and that one of the diaspora's greatest contributions is a lasting belief in what is possible.

Rachel Lebaredian

Co-Founder

Co-Founder & Director · Rev & Rachel Foundation

LinkedIn

Rachel Lebaredian brings to the foundation a deep commitment to education, community, and the enduring value of philanthropy rooted in human potential. As Co‑Founder and Director of the Rev and Rachel Foundation, she helps shape its vision, partnerships, and grantmaking, with a focus on expanding educational opportunity, supporting youth development, and strengthening the institutions and initiatives that serve the next generation.

Her work is grounded in a long‑standing belief that creativity, culture, and access to opportunity are essential to the health and resilience of communities. Through the foundation, she has helped advance initiatives in Armenia, New Orleans, and other places important to the family, with an emphasis on education, creative and STEM learning, and clear pathways for young people to thrive.

Rachel brings thoughtful leadership, discernment, and a deep commitment to values‑driven philanthropy. She believes that lasting impact is built through sustained partnership, strategic investment, and an unwavering conviction in the potential of young people and the communities that support them.
